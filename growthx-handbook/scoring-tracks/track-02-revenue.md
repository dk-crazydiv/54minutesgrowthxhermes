# Track 02 · Revenue — rubric

← [Scoring framework](../04-scoring.md) | [Index](../README.md)

**208 base points + overflow (uncapped).** Root parameter: **signups, 20x.** 100% live product. No decks.

Real demand in an 8-hour window is rare, so the rubric weights observable signals: signups, product quality, and real money moved. The VC-lens parameters (business impact, right to win, why now, moat) stay in as directional signals but carry lower weight.

## The parameters

### 1. Signups — 20x · max 80 (root)
Email + first-use event (created an account, generated an output, ran the core flow). Team members / anonymous visits do not count.

| Level | Band | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| L1 | 0 | No external email + first-use event exists. Traffic without accounts is still zero. |
| L2 | 1 to 25 | A handful of real outsiders signed up and triggered a first-use event, mostly via DMs. |
| L3 | 26 to 100 | Growth moved past personal begging into at least one channel that converts. |
| L4 | 101 to 250 | Real distribution — multiple channels or one that caught fire; activation holds up. |
| L5 | 251+ | Signups compound without the team pushing; referral visible. **Overflow: beyond 250, +1 pt × 20x per additional 50 signups.** |

### 2. Live product quality — 8x · max 32
Time to first value, task completion rate, UX craft, perceived differentiation.

| Level | Name | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| L1 | Broken | Deployed product can't deliver its promise even once. A demo video standing in for a live app counts as broken. |
| L2 | Rough MVP, happy path only | One narrow path delivers value; any deviation breaks it. |
| L3 | Working product, does what it claims | A cold user reaches first value unassisted in a couple of minutes. |
| L4 | Polished, noticeably better than alternatives | Craft shows; output beats the incumbent tool without the team explaining. |
| L5 | 10x product, magical onboarding | Feels like a funded team's public launch; mentor can't tell it was built in 8 hours. |

### 3. Revenue generated (USD) — 12x · max 48
Real money moved during the event. Stripe, Razorpay, any payment processor. Not services.

| Level | Band | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| L1 | $0 | No completed payment, or only teammate/friend/service payments. |
| L2 | Up to $25 | One or a few real payments for product access; passes the removal test. |
| L3 | $25 to $100 | Several independent buyers paid a stated price. |
| L4 | $100 to $500 | Repeatable path from stranger to paid — buyers arrived through the funnel. |
| L5 | $500+ | Serious money from a real market, spread across buyers. Mentors audit legitimacy. **Overflow: beyond $500, +1 pt × 12x per additional $100.** |

### 4. Waitlist — 4x · max 16
Email drop on a landing page; user has not touched the product. Lower weight than signups (intent without commitment).

| Level | Band | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| L1 | 0 | No email capture, or nobody outside the team dropped an email. |
| L2 | 1 to 50 | A trickle of real emails before product access, mostly personal reach-outs. |
| L3 | 51 to 250 | Landing page converts people from a real channel, not just founders' contacts. |
| L4 | 251 to 1,000 | Demand implying a real channel win or a mobilizable audience. |
| L5 | 1,000+ | Four-figure demand in 8 hours. Mentors discount manufactured volume. **Overflow: beyond 1k, +1 pt × 4x per additional 250 entries.** |

### 5. Business impact — 4x · max 16
The money math: who pays for this problem, how often, and what moves when the product handles it.

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| L1 | No business case articulated. |
| L2 | Weak case: likely below 5% projected movement. |
| L3 | Clear case: 5–10% projected movement on one meaningful metric, math shown. |
| L4 | Strong case: 10–30% projected movement, metric + baseline + prize named. |
| L5 | Top-priority case: 30%+ movement on a top metric, path to material impact. |

### 6. Right to win — 2x · max 8
Founder-market fit + insight. Unfair advantage = 10x better shot than a random team.

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| L1 | Team could be anyone. |
| L2 | Generic interest in the space. |
| L3 | Some domain exposure (internship, freelance, family business, serious hobby). |
| L4 | Direct operator or domain experience; a clear non-obvious insight encoded in the build. |
| L5 | Deep founder-market fit; unfair advantage visible in the build itself (proprietary data, distribution, judgment). |

### 7. Why now — 1x · max 4
Weak: "AI is hot." Strong: a specific recent unlock (capability, regulation, behavior shift).

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| L1 | Could have been built 5 years ago. |
| L2 | Riding general trends (a macro wave, not a mechanism). |
| L3 | Clear tailwind in last 2 years, linked causally. |
| L4 | Specific, verifiable unlock in the last 12 months without which the product couldn't exist. |
| L5 | Window opened under 6 months ago, visible in the product (e.g. in the unit economics). |

### 8. Moat and defensibility — 1x · max 4
Taste counts as moat when it shows up in product craft (Linear, Superhuman).

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| L1 | Copyable in a weekend. |
| L2 | Thin, first-mover only. |
| L3 | Workflow lock-in, integrations, taste. |
| L4 | Data flywheel, network effects, switching costs — at least one compounding mechanism live. |
| L5 | Compounding moat: proprietary data + network effects strengthen with scale, reinforcing each other. |

## Revenue total
`80 + 32 + 48 + 16 + 8 + 8 + 8 + 4 + 4 = 208 base points.` Signups, revenue generated, and waitlist overflow on top, uncapped.

## What counts as revenue
Money earned from selling a **product**, not your team's time.
- **Qualifies:** paid signups (Stripe, Razorpay, one-time or subscription), API/usage fees, paid digital goods, premium upgrades.
- **Does not qualify:** consulting/agency/done-for-you fees, human-in-the-loop work the team performs manually, payments from team members or friends, gifts reframed as revenue.
- **The test:** if you removed the product tomorrow, does the revenue also disappear? If yes, it counts.
